Prof Dora Akunyili

The woman who fought against fake drugs

A short biography

  • Birth: On 14th July 1954, the family of Chief and Mrs. Paul Young Edemobi welcomed the birth of their daughter, Dora
  • Education:
      - FSLC (1966): St. Patrick's Primary School, Isuofia, Anambra State
      - WASC (1973): Queen of the Rosary Secondary School Nsukka, Enugu State
      - B.Pharm (1978): University of Nigeria, Nsukka (U.N.N)
      - Ph.D. in Ethnopharmacology (1985): University of Nigeria, Nsukka
  • Career:
      1981, Graduate Assistant in the FaU.NN. of Pharmaceutical Sciences, U.N.N
      1990, Senior Lecturer in the FaU.NN. of Pharmaceutical Sciences, U.N.N
      1996, Consultant Pharmacologist at the College of Medicine, U.N.N
      1996, Appointed Zonal Secretary of the Petroleum Special Trust Fund (P.T.F)
      2001-2009, Appointed Director-General of the National Agency for Food and Drug Administration and Control (NAFDAC)
      2008-2010, Appointed Minister of Information and Communications
  • Fight against fake drugs:
      (1) 2001, she started a war against counterfeit drugs that saw many open-air medicine markets across the country closed down
      (2) In Kano State after her officers confiscated Â£140,000 worth of fake drugs
      (3) July 2003, in a case of counterfeit medical supplies she confiscated supplies and found fake adrenalin, fake muscle relaxant and infected intravenous drips
      (4) June 2006, she was reported to have secured convictions for 45 counterfeiters with 56 cases pending
      (5) As NAFDAC DG, she made the public aware of the dangers of substandard drugs and encouraged people to report suspicious drugs
  • Death: On 7th June 2014, the whole world mourned the death of a great woman after batteling with uterine cancer
